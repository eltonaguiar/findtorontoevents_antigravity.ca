Core Methodology: Technical Analysis in Crypto

Cryptocurrency markets behave differently from traditional equities. Our approach is grounded in these observations:

  • Crypto trends persistently. Unlike stocks where mean reversion works 60% of the time, crypto assets exhibit strong momentum effects. BTC went from $3K to $69K to $16K to $73K -- these are multi-month trends, not noise.
  • Technical analysis works better in crypto. Due to high retail participation and fewer institutional stabilizers, technical patterns (support/resistance, moving averages, Ichimoku) have higher signal-to-noise ratios than in equities.
  • 24/7 markets amplify edge. Unlike stocks with 6.5 hours of trading, crypto runs 24/7. Algorithms that capture overnight moves have a structural advantage over human traders who sleep.
  • Volatility is the product, not the bug. Crypto volatility creates opportunity. Our Ichimoku and StochRSI algorithms are designed for volatile, trending environments.

Key Concepts

ICHIMOKU CLOUD
Origins: Goichi Hosoda (1960s, Japan) -- A comprehensive indicator combining 5 lines (Tenkan-sen, Kijun-sen, Senkou Span A/B, Chikou Span) into a visual "cloud" that shows support, resistance, momentum, and trend direction simultaneously. Originally designed for Japanese rice futures. Adapted to crypto where its multi-timeframe nature captures both trends and reversals.
STOCHASTIC RSI
Combines RSI with Stochastic Oscillator -- StochRSI applies the Stochastic formula to RSI values instead of price, creating a more sensitive oscillator. In crypto, standard RSI often stays overbought during strong trends. StochRSI crossovers (K crossing above D) provide earlier entry signals during momentum moves.
TREND FOLLOWING
Sharpe 4.45 in Backtest (A+ Grade) -- Classic momentum strategy: buy when price is above moving averages and making higher highs, sell when trend breaks. Works exceptionally well in crypto due to strong trending behavior. Used by major crypto funds (Alameda, Jump Trading). Our highest-scoring backtest strategy.
MEAN REVERSION
Sharpe -1.56 in Backtest (D Grade) -- Buy oversold assets expecting them to bounce. Works in range-bound equity markets but fails catastrophically in trending crypto markets. Crypto can stay "oversold" for months during bear markets. Our worst-performing strategy on crypto -- included here as a cautionary example.

Market Inefficiencies in Crypto

MOMENTUM EFFECT
Crypto Momentum is Persistent (Liu et al. 2019) -- Unlike equities where momentum reverses at 12+ months, crypto momentum persists strongly at 1-4 week horizons. Winners keep winning. This is why our Trend Following strategy scored A+ and Mean Reversion scored D.
CROSS-EXCHANGE ARBITRAGE
Price Discrepancies Across Exchanges -- BTC price on Binance, Coinbase, Kraken, and Crypto.com can differ by 0.1-2% during volatile periods. High-frequency traders exploit these gaps. We currently use only one exchange -- a major missed opportunity.
WEEKEND EFFECTS
Lower Liquidity = Bigger Moves -- Crypto trading volume drops 20-40% on weekends. Reduced liquidity means larger price moves from smaller orders. Algorithms running 24/7 capture weekend dislocations that human traders miss.
SENTIMENT DRIVEN
Social Media Moves Markets -- A single Elon Musk tweet moved DOGE 30% in hours. Crypto Fear & Greed Index extremes (below 20 or above 80) historically correlate with reversals. We currently have zero sentiment data integration.

Academic Research Sources

Liu, Tsyvinski & Wu (2019)
"Common Risk Factors in Cryptocurrency" -- Identified cryptocurrency-specific momentum factor. Found that a momentum strategy yields 25% monthly alpha. Foundational work for crypto quantitative strategies.
Goichi Hosoda (1960s-1969)
Creator of the Ichimoku Kinko Hyo system. Originally designed for Japanese rice trading. The "one glance equilibrium chart" provides support, resistance, momentum, and trend direction in a single view. Adapted globally for all asset classes.
Baur & Dimpfl (2021)
"The Volatility of Bitcoin and its Role as a Medium of Exchange and a Store of Value" -- Demonstrated that BTC volatility is fundamentally different from equity volatility, exhibiting clustering and long-memory effects that trend-following algorithms can exploit.
Cong, He, Li & Wei (2022)
"Crypto Wash Trading" -- Found that up to 70% of reported crypto volume is wash trading. This means real liquidity is lower than it appears, making slippage modeling critical for realistic backtesting. Our configurable slippage parameter addresses this.
