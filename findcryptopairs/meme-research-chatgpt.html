⚠️ Critical Issues Identified P0

1. Statistical Invalidity (Sample Size Crisis)

With only 20 resolved signals, your measured performance cannot distinguish between a "broken" system and a "weak but fixable" one. To estimate a 40% win rate with ±5 percentage point precision, you need ~350-400 resolved signals. The current "20 resolved" is an early pilot, not a production inference base.

Wilson Score Interval: For 1 win / 19 losses, the 95% confidence interval is approximately 0.9% to 23.6%. The probability that the true win rate is already near 40% is negligible.

2. Feature Conflicts (Momentum vs Mean Reversion)

Your framework has conflicting heuristics: Momentum says "positive 24h change is good" (target +3-15%), but Entry Position says "near the 24h low is better entry." In strong breakouts, the best momentum entries often occur near the high, not the low. The system risks selecting trades that are neither good momentum entries nor good reversals.

3. Microstructure Blind Spots (Depth Missing)

Spread alone is misleading in fast meme-coin moves. An order book can look tight while being thin. Slippage and adverse selection are driven by depth, not just top-of-book spread. Kraken itself monitors depth for healthy markets—your framework doesn't.

4. Survivorship Bias (Kraken Universe)

The Kraken ranking universe is already a filtered set (Kraken's evaluation screens for security, compliance, liquidity). Any backtest using only currently-listed coins without accounting for delistings or newly listed assets will overstate robustness.

5. No Falsifiable Definition of Success

The Kraken ranking model lacks tracked forward performance. You cannot improve what you don't measure. The core change needed: turn "a dashboard that ranks coins" into "a decision system with testable forecasts."
