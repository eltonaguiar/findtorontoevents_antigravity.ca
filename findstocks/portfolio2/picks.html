Understanding Stock Picks iWhat each metric means and how picks are selected.

▼
Score
A composite rating (0-100) based on multiple factors: momentum, value, growth, and technical strength. Higher score = more factors aligned bullishly. Scores are recalculated nightly.
Target Price
The algorithm's estimated fair value. If the current price is below the target, the stock is considered undervalued. The gap between current and target represents the expected upside.
Momentum
How strongly the stock has been trending recently. Positive momentum means the price has been consistently rising. It measures the speed and direction of price changes.
Risk Level
How volatile the stock is. Low = stable blue-chip (like JNJ, WMT). Medium = normal volatility. High = can swing 3-5%+ daily (like NVDA, NFLX). Higher risk = higher potential reward but also larger losses.
SL / TP / Max Hold
SL (Stop Loss) = sell if down X%. TP (Take Profit) = sell if up X%. Max Hold = sell after N days regardless. These define the exit strategy for each pick.
Win Rate
Historical percentage of picks from this algorithm that hit their take profit before hitting their stop loss. A 70% win rate means 7 of 10 past picks were profitable.
