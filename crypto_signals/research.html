Consensus Across All Models

All 5 AI models agree on these points:
  • A generic system cannot optimally trade all crypto assets
  • Hybrid architecture (shared framework + asset-specific modules) is optimal
  • Momentum works for BTC/ETH; different approach needed for altcoins
  • Walk-forward validation and no look-ahead bias are essential
  • Transaction costs and slippage significantly impact real performance
  • Risk management (position sizing, stops) matters more than signal quality

Lessons Learned

  • V2 trailing stop destroyed performance — moving SL to breakeven too early caused premature exits on volatile crypto assets
  • V2 separate strategy sets added noise — mean-reversion strategies fire too many low-quality signals on AVAX
  • V3 bear market filter helped slightly — reduced AVAX signals from 54 to 34, but the remaining signals still lost money
  • V1 remains the best for BTC and BNB — the original confluence approach with 12 strategies is already well-tuned
  • AVAX is fundamentally unsuitable for momentum-based confluence signals — Kimi's -0.36 correlation confirmed across all 3 engine versions
Honest Verdict: The V1 Confluence Engine works for BTC (+129%), BNB (+131%), and ETH (+80%). AVAX should be excluded from momentum-based signal generation until a dedicated mean-reversion engine with on-chain data (subnet announcements, developer activity) is built. The system is deployed via GitHub Actions (Confluence_Signal_Backtest) for daily automated backtesting.

Conclusions

What We Proved

  1. Momentum strategies work on BTC and ETH — RSI Momentum (Sharpe 1.26 on BTC), MTF Momentum (Sharpe 1.24 on AVAX but with caveats), Donchian Breakout (Sharpe 1.07 on BNB)
  2. Mean reversion is needed for AVAX and BNB — Kimi's empirical data shows -0.36 and -0.28 momentum correlations respectively
  3. Confluence signals (multiple strategies agreeing) improve reliability — but only when the right strategies are applied to the right assets
  4. ATR-based TP/SL provides dynamic risk management — adapts to volatility regime automatically
  5. Bootstrap CIs confirm 3 of 13 strategies are statistically significant on AVAX (MTF Momentum, RSI Momentum, Donchian Breakout)
  6. Year-by-year analysis reveals the real edge is bear market protection — MTF Momentum on AVAX: +56.7% in 2022 vs buy-and-hold -90.5%

What We Learned from Failures

  • V1 Confluence on AVAX failed because we applied momentum strategies to a mean-reverting asset
  • Win rates of 33-40% are normal for trend-following — the edge comes from avg_win > avg_loss, not from high win rate
  • SL too tight for crypto volatility — 2x ATR is insufficient for AVAX (91% annualized vol); need 2.5-3x ATR
  • 4H signal confidence is sobering — win rates hover 48-52% on short timeframes; edge compounds over hundreds of trades

Honest Risk Assessment

  • Max drawdowns of -40% to -60% are possible even with the best strategies
  • AVAX can drop -21% to -34% after a buy signal fires
  • Past performance does NOT guarantee future results — crypto market structure is evolving rapidly
  • Alpha decays: signals lose ~50% of edge within 6-12 months of becoming public
  • Slippage on AVAX ($18.7M daily volume) can be 50-100bps vs 5-10bps on BTC
